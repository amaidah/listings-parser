      This is one of my favorite dumpling places to go to. Before when I had come to the Westfield Santa Anita mall, I used to dread eating there. The original food court was just like any other mall and even though there were a lot of choices, that quality of the food was mediocre. Then they opened up the 2nd food court and I saw that there was a Din Tai Fung there and I couldn't wait to try it! As with every Din Tai Fung, the wait is exceptionally long, unless you come with a small party. After waiting for about an hour and a half, my wife and I was seated and we placed our order. The staff was very friendly, constantly kept an eye out on our table to make sure that were satisfied with everything and that we had everything we needed to enjoy our meal. Highly recommend visiting this location as they also offer bar seating, which is first come first serve, so if you got a group and you see 4 open seats, go for it and sit down. Skip that 2 hour wait. Sadly I found this out AFTER I was seated.

      This review isn't about the food. I had my mother with me and we couldn't wait 2 hours for a table. This is a review and kudos for their 21st-century reservation system. AND a helpful tip for all who long to dine here...but you arrived at dinnertime!!! HINT: If you want to eat here, do something I didn't know to do...that is, stop by the restaurant when you first come to the Westfield Santa Anita Mall...and at the first reservation desk, find out how long the wait...then give them your cell phone number. Then go shop!!! Chances are the wait will be loooonnnnng, and you have plenty of time to shop leisurely....You will get two texts...the first is a confirmation that you have a reservation and it informs you that a second will be on the way. Then a second text comes telling you that you are now at the top of the wait list, and to get your booty over to the restaurant. So. Two areas at the restaurant. One to place your reservation and input your cell phone for notification. The second after you get your text telling you that you are at the top of the waiting list. How do people without cellphones make a reservation??? Good Question. I'd love to know what happens to those folks...I guess they have to waste valuable shopping time... Anyway, Four stars for being tech savvy. Love it. Minus one star because even people who don't have cell phones deserve XLB (that's xiao lung bao, ya'll....juicy soup dumplings, without the soup)!!

      Service - excellent customer service, hosts and servers were all very polite and attentive they were also quick with making sure all our orders were arriving in a timely manner. Although we did go on a weekday when there was no wait... so I can't speak for the service you may receive during weekends. Food - they're known for their xiao long bao. It is in a thinner wrap and they all come warm and not broken. That means each little xiao long bao is filled with delicious dumpling soup when you eat it. The black sesame bun dessert is also really good! They can wait to send it out so that when you are ready for them they come out warm and fresh! Restaurant - This ding tai fung is huge !       When I first went to Din Tai Fung in Arcadia 2 years ago, I had been told the hype from relatives about the legendary soup dumplings, and how they're internationally renowned and earned the chain a Michelin star. When I went to the first Arcadia location at that time, I was not too impressed with their menu selection, although their soup dumplings weren't too bad. Flash forward 2 years later, and Din Tai Fung has now opened a location in Westfield Santa Anita Mall. I visited the new location with some friends and I noticed the menu has expanded significantly. There are new flavors of xiao long bao, including red bean, taro, and chocolate with mochi. Being as adventurous as I am, I decided to give the chocolate mochi a try. I also gave the sea salt black tea a try. The sea salt black tea was very good, sweet but not too sweet. The chocolate xiao long bao were absolutely divine. The order only comes with 5 pieces (as opposed to 10 for xiao long bao), and I soon figured out why. The chocolate is very gooey and melted, but yet the XLB isn't so hot that you can't just bite into it. The mochi adds just a slight bit of texture. The flavor was sweet, but again not too sweet. I also got to try some of my friend's shrimp wonton, which was also very delicious. Albeit not as good as XLB, still tasty. Although I wasn't planning on going to Din Tai Fung again because I was not too impressed with their XLB and menu 2 years ago, their expanded menu with new selections has me interested and I do plan on making a repeat trip in the future.
